Technical Indicators

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

  • > 70: overbought
  • < 30: oversold (potential buy zone)

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

  • If MACD moves above the signal line → bullish signal.

EMA (Exponential Moving Average)

  • 5‑day EMA above 20‑day EMA → upward trend.
  • 5‑day EMA below 20‑day EMA → downward trend (look for more confirmation).

Gauging Market Sentiment

  • Watch for major events: scandals, elections, wars.
  • VIX: rising VIX usually means markets are nervous.
  • Put vs. Call ratio: high put volume often signals bearish sentiment or rising fear.
  • Monitor news and trends regularly.

Economic Influence (2.4)

  • GDP: do you see an increasing trend?
  • Unemployment: what is the trend over time?
  • Inflation: which direction is it moving?
  • Trade wars or major policy shifts?
  • Use Google Alerts for key numbers and reports.

Test Your Knowledge

1. Watch 5 Companies

Pick five companies from different industries. Track both their news and price movements over time.

2. Deep Dive on 1 Stock

  • Check P/E, Price‑to‑Book, and Debt‑to‑Equity.
  • Ask: is it fairly valued?
  • Look for trends, patterns, and support & resistance levels.
  • Track it over time and compare your thesis to reality.

3. Use Your Skills to Pick Winners

Combine fundamentals, charts, and sentiment to build your own watchlist and thesis.

4. Manage Risk

Use stop losses or protective puts to limit downside risk.

Smart Options (3.1)

Review option contract terms before trading.

Basic Positions

  • Short Call: You sold the call and received premium.
  • Long Call: Bullish; you expect price to rise (right to buy at a strike price).
  • Short Put: You sold the put and received premium.
  • Long Put: Bearish; you expect price to drop.
